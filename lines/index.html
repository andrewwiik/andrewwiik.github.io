So what even is a line?

According to the online art center....

“Line is a one dimensional element measured only in length.
It is an abstract concept that is more perceived than actually viewed.
Very few lines appear in nature, yet we see the edges of things around us, helping us to differentiate a shape or form from its surroundings. ”

Now lets explore the different types of lines

Contour Lines

Lines that are used to define edges

Dividing Lines

Lines that can be used to define edges but are most commonly used to divide space

Decoration Lines

A decoration line is used to embellish a object

Gesture Lines

are quick and rough continuous lines used to capture form and movement.

A Line can come in many differnt forms

  • Horizontal
  • Vertical
  • Diagonal
  • Curved

A line can be where a drawing starts or it can tie everything together into a work of art.

What are Lines used for?

Lines are used to create shape, pattern, texture, space, movement. and optical illusion in design. A line can express various moods and feelings.

Every line tells us something

A line can tell us more about than just the edge of something

Thin Lines

Thin lines are fragile and delicate and give off an elegant quality.

Thick Lines

Thick lines are bold and suggest strength.

Horizontal Lines

Horizontal lines are parallel to the horizon and look like they are lying down at rest.they suggest calm quiet and a relaxed comfort.

Vertical Lines

Vertical lines are perpendicular to the horizon and are filled with potential energy. These lines represent rigidness and stability.

Diagonal Lines

Diagonal Lines are unbalanced and are filled with uncontrolled energy.

Curved Lines

Curved lines are softer than straight lines they are less predictable than straight lines.they can be calm or dynamic depending on how much they curve.

Exact Lines

Exact lines are a combination of diagonal lines that connect points.they create excitement and intense movement.
